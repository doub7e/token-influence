# Qwen3-1.7B-Base GRPO Math Training: Entropy Stabilization

**Date:** 2026-03-04
**Commit:** `bf8e43f`

## Summary

Systematic hyperparameter search to train Qwen3-1.7B-Base (pure base model, no instruct FT) with GRPO on math problems. The core challenge is **entropy collapse**: without instruction tuning, the base model's entropy rapidly collapses under GRPO, causing degenerate outputs. After 8 experiments, we found that **entropy_coeff=0.01 + kl_loss_coef=0.05** stabilizes entropy and achieves strong MATH-500 results.

## MATH-500 Evaluation Results

| Model | pass@1 | pass@4 | Δ pass@1 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Qwen3-1.7B-Base (original) | 36.4% | 65.0% | — |
| ent015-kl05 (step 400, entropy drifted to 6-7) | 55.4% | 71.0% | +19.0% |
| **ent01-kl05 (step 200, entropy stable ~2)** | **61.7%** | **76.0%** | **+25.3%** |

## Full Experiment Log

### Phase 1: No entropy bonus → immediate collapse

| # | Experiment | entropy_coeff | kl_loss_coef | clip | resp_len | Steps | Entropy Trajectory | Outcome |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | qwen3-1.7b-base-math | 0 | 0.001 | 0.2 | 1024 | ~183 | 1.5 → 0.05 | Collapsed: ultra-short outputs guessing \boxed{answer} |
| 2 | qwen3-1.7b-base-math-long | 0 | 0.001 | 0.2 | 4096 | ~63 | 1.95 → 0.075 | Collapsed: repetitive loops (same expr 150+ times) |

**Finding:** Base model without any entropy regularization collapses within 50-100 steps.

### Phase 2: Entropy coefficient sweep → unstable

| # | Experiment | entropy_coeff | kl_loss_coef | clip | Steps | Entropy Trajectory | Outcome |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 3 | ent01 | 0.01 | 0.001 | 0.2 | ~101 | 1.95 → 0.128 | Still collapsed; 0.01 insufficient |
| 4 | ent02 | 0.02 | 0.001 | 0.2 | ~191 | 1.95 → 9+ | Entropy too high, model too random, score stagnated ~0.2 |
| 5 | ent015 | 0.015 | 0.001 | 0.2 | ~225 | 1.95 → 2-4 → 8+ | Good initially, entropy exploded after step 180 |
| 6 | ent015-clip01 | 0.015 | 0.001 | 0.1 | ~172 | 1.95 → 3-4.5 → 6.8 | Slower drift with smaller clip, but still exploded |

**Finding:** Entropy bonus alone is an unstable equilibrium — constant upward pressure with no anchor. 0.01 too weak, 0.02 too strong, 0.015 works short-term but drifts long-term. Clip ratio slows drift but cannot prevent it.

### Phase 3: KL anchoring → stable training

| # | Experiment | entropy_coeff | kl_loss_coef | clip | Steps | Entropy Trajectory | Outcome |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 7 | ent015-kl05 | 0.015 | 0.05 | 0.2 | ~435 | 1.95 → 3-5 → 6-7 | KL slowed drift but entropy_coeff=0.015 still too strong |
| 8 | **ent01-kl05** | **0.01** | **0.05** | 0.2 | ~272 | 1.95 → 1-2.3 → **stable 2-3** | **Best: entropy stable, pass@1=61.7%** |

**Finding:** KL loss (kl_loss_coef=0.05) provides a fixed anchor to the reference model (entropy ~1.5-2.0). Combined with moderate entropy bonus (0.01), entropy stabilizes around 2-3. The KL contribution is now ~10-30% of PG loss (vs negligible 0.03% at kl_loss_coef=0.001).

## Key Insights

1. **Base model GRPO needs dual regularization**: entropy bonus (upward force) + KL loss (anchor to reference). Neither alone is sufficient.
2. **KL anchoring is critical**: Without it, entropy bonus creates runaway drift. kl_loss_coef must be 50-100x larger than default (0.05 vs 0.001) to have any effect.
3. **Entropy bonus should be conservative**: 0.01 works better than 0.015 when paired with KL=0.05. Lower entropy bonus = less upward pressure = easier for KL to anchor.
4. **Base model has latent math ability**: Qwen3-1.7B-Base pass@4=65% on MATH-500, suggesting it knows math but lacks consistent output formatting. GRPO effectively teaches output consistency (+25% pass@1).
5. **Reward shaping gap**: Current reward = binary correctness + overlong penalty. Repetition metrics are logged but NOT penalized. Adding repetition penalty to reward could further improve.

## Best Configuration

```
entropy_coeff=0.01
kl_loss_coef=0.05
kl_loss_type=low_var_kl
clip_ratio=0.2
lr=1e-6
response_length=4096
n_resp_per_prompt=16
train_prompt_bsz=64
```
